How like a winter hath my absence been From thee, the pleasure of
the fleeting year! What freezings have I felt, what dark
days seen! What old December's bareness everywhere! And yet
this time removed was summer's time; The teeming autumn,
big with rich increase, Bearing the wanton burden of the prime,
Like widow'd wombs after their lords' decease: Yet this
abundant issue seemed to me But hope of orphans, and unfathered
fruit; For summer and his pleasures wait on thee, And,
thou away, the very birds are mute: Or, if they sing,
'tis with so dull a cheer, That leaves look pale, dreading the
winter's near.
